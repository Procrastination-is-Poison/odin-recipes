Coconut-Lime Salmon

Description

This is a very quick, easy low-carb recipe, rich and filling without being too heavy. Ginger, basil & lime juice serve to brighten everything up!

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp. coconut oil, divided
  • 4 skinless salmon fillets
  • 1 tsp. kosher salt, divided
  • 1/2 a small onion, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 1/2" piece ginger, grated
  • 1 can Thai Kitchen coconut milk
  • 1 tbsp. fresh lime juice, plus lime wedges for serving
  • 2 tbsp. chopped fresh basil
  • 1 Fresno chilli, seeded & chopped

Steps

  1. In a large cast-iron skillet, heat 1 tablespoon oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Season the salmon with ½ teaspoon salt.
  3. When oil is hot but not smoking, add salmon skin side up. Let sit undisturbed until golden and salmon easily releases from pan, about 4 minutes
  4. Use a thin metal spatula, flip salmon and cook until golden on the other side, about 2 minutes more.
  5. Remove to a plate
  6. Reduce heat to medium and add remaining tablespoon oil and onion to skillet. Cook, stirring occasionally, until softened, 2 minutes
  7. Add garlic and ginger and cook, stirring, until fragrant, 1 minute
  8. Stir in coconut milk and bring to a simmer
  9. Cook, stirring occasionally, until thickened, 5 minutes. Stir in lime juice and remaining ½ teaspoon salt
  10. Remove from heat and return salmon to pan. Garnish with basil and Fresno chili, and serve with lime wedges
